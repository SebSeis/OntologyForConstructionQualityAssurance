Evaluation

Automated consistency checking
The consistency of the OCQA ontology was checked using the Pellet reasoner. The Pellet reasoner is an open OWL-DL reasoner used for inference and consistency checking of ontologies. Pellet supports reasoning for individuals, cardinality restrictions, custom data types, and more (pellet ref). The check with Pellet showed that the created ontology is consistent and coherent.

Furthermore, the ontology was checked for possible modeling errors using the OntOlogy Pitfall Scanner. The OOPS Pitfall Scanner was developed to automatically detect certain errors in ontologies created based on OOPS principles (Object-Oriented and Ontological Principles). Among other things, it can identify problems such as inconsistent or redundantly defined classes, missing hierarchical relationships, and inconsistent property expressions. The scanner's checklist can be viewed at https://oops.linkeddata.es/catalogue.jsp. It is a useful tool for improving the quality of OCQA and detecting potential errors early on (oops ref)
